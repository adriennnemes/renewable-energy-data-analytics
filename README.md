# Renewable Energy Data Analytics

> **Note**
>
> This repository contains a two-member team project developed as part of the MSc Computer Science (Data Science) programme at FH Wiener Neustadt.


## Project Overview

This project explores long-term trends in renewable energy production and CO₂ emissions using publicly available datasets from **Our World in Data (OWID)**.

The objective was to investigate whether increasing the share of renewable electricity generation is associated with lower CO₂ emissions per capita.

Using exploratory data analysis, statistical interpretation and modern data visualisation techniques, we analysed multiple countries over more than three decades and communicated the findings through static and animated visualisations.


## Research Question

**Does a higher share of renewable electricity automatically lead to lower CO₂ emissions?**

Our analysis shows that while renewable energy generally contributes to reducing emissions, the relationship is influenced by several additional factors, including nuclear energy, economic structure and overall energy demand.
